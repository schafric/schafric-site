[Super interesting podcast](https://open.spotify.com/episode/1WICjyeivPI7KqGFq4zFKe?si=211b936732ed4d18) about Google's approach to AI and a story how their Transformer paper ('attention is all you need'), which became the foundational blueprint for virtually all modern large language models, enabled the modern AI era. Worth a listen, basically every minute is filled with interesting insights.

For example, did you know that:
- Google found a clever approach to recruiting academic AI-researchers into Google? They offered them part time job while being able to keep their academics work as well - just giving them slight exposure to Google, which gradually drew them in.
- There were 8 people who wrote the Transformer paper, enabling LLMs as we know them today, all of them worked at Google.
- That paper is from 2017 and even though the paper mentions how useful it can be for Google products, Google leadership did basically nothing with it for a long time, despite the team being very vocal about it. They sat on it until the researchers got frustrated and left to build their own things or join OpenAI: open AI launched ChatGPT, Google realized they were in a dire situation, and started scrambling. Google than bought one company of one researcher for $2.000.000.000 to get back into the game and get that one researcher back to Google.
- ChatGPT caused 'Code Red' at Google - basically realising how far behind they are behind and shifting everyone in the company on Gemini and it's integration into Google products. They developed and launched Gemini in ~13 months!
- There is a guy called Jeff Dean, who is known in Google as Chuck Norris of engineering. He is repeatedly able to do incredible things - like jump into a product he's never seen before and optimize its most challenging problem by 20x, for example 😀 are even "chuck norris" style jokes about him:
- To Jeff Dean, "NP" means "No Problemo".
    - Jeff Dean's PIN is the last 4 digits of pi.
    - Jeff Dean once bit a spider, the spider got super powers and C++ readability.
    - Jeff Dean's resume has so many accomplishments, it has a table of contents.
- Google bought DeepMind for a few hundred million dollars, extremely upsetting Elon Musk and Mark Zuckerberg, who both wanted to acquire it too. Today, it's worth around $6 billion.

And a lot more, it's just packed with astonishing stories. Yes, it's 4 hours podcast, but it's really worth it I think.
