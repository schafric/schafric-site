He, and the whole Shopify, does things quite differently and have bold opinions - and it's really interesting to hear.
Some interesting stuff from the podcast:
- He, even as CEO of multi-thousand people company, still regularly codes.
- He wrote himself a small interceptor for his computer, which tracks EVERYTHING he writes and puts it into some files. He has a history of 15-20 years of all stuff he ever wrote. All his opinions, all his thoughts, all his emails, messages - you get it. I see it as quite a cool idea!
- He deliberately uses beta-softwares on his devices. Including beta-iOS etc. Imagine CEO of such a huge company not being able to call someone due to bug in the phone! He has a good reason for it though.
- He believes that "leadership" and "consensus" are the ends of the same spectrum. Meaning: either you have consensus or leadership. He says that consensus in a group to make a decision, it's in an absence of leadership - everyone in the group abdicated on their 'leadership' to read leadership (yes, quite ["founder mode"](https://paulgraham.com/foundermode.html))
- He strongly believes in learning potential with video games - you can learn, in a fun way, a lot of stuff while playing a game.
